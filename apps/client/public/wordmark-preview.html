Reference. Filled silhouette, straight lines only, wing-tip serifs that extend past the body. Vocabulary the rest of the family inherits.

Declyne#6b5a9e
eclyne

Faceted D with the bowl peak shifted UP into the upper third — short rise, long descent. The asymmetric bowl IS a decline curve. Same wing-tip serifs as Waymark on the spine corners.

Sygnalist#6AD7A3
ygnalist

Angular S built from straight horizontals and verticals only — five bars stacked. The letter itself reads as a signal trace stepping through three levels. No decoration; the form is the metaphor.

Bridgefour#c8552e
ridgefour

Angular B — two arches with sharp peaks, atop a vertical pier. Bottom arch larger than top, like a real B. The whole thing reads as a two-arch bridge cross-section. Burnt orange.
